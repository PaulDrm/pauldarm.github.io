Why you should start feeling stupid – and why that is hopefully not a bad thing

Last week was one for the history books in the advancement of AI. Both OpenAI and Google released their latest model updates, passing the most important current benchmark in AI: the vibe check. As I'm working in this field, I'd like to share my thoughts on why AI progress is accelerating, and why most people will soon feel "stupid" compared to state-of-the-art generative AI models.

Recent AI Advancements

On March 25th, OpenAI released a new version of their image generation model SORA integrated with ChatGPT-4o, capable of hyper-realistic photos and advanced instruction-following. See examples at https://sora.com.

Example image generated by OpenAI's new image generation tool.

Just one day later, Google released their model Gemini-2.5-pro-exp-03-25, achieving top scores in reasoning tasks on Livebench and outperforming recent models like Claude-3-7 and Deepseek-R1 significantly.

Livebench results from 31.03.2025

Impact on Jobs and Society

Generative AI, such as ChatGPT-4o, directly competes with graphic designers, digital artists, photo editors, and even software developers. An example of this trend is the recent "Ghiblivication," inspired by Studio Ghibli’s distinctive style, where anyone can instantly transform ideas into Ghibli-styled visuals.

"Ghiblivication" examples using ChatGPT-4o image generation.

Historically, we've seen technology surpass human capabilities, exemplified by AlphaGo defeating Lee Sedol, who later retired, recognizing he couldn't compete against an unbeatable entity.

Future Outlook

The trajectory of AI growth suggests increasingly intelligent models. Experts like Bill Gates and Geoffrey Hinton acknowledge that these models might soon surpass human intelligence, triggering societal shifts similar to the Industrial Revolution.

The Iron Rolling Mill (Modern Cyclopes), painting by Adolph Menzel.

Possible Solutions

To manage this shift positively, one proposal is a tax on AI usage, redistributing profits to society, akin to a basic universal income. This could ensure everyone benefits from AI, preventing a scenario where only the wealthy gain significantly.

Conclusion

Generative AI is reshaping our world rapidly. It’s important not to underestimate its potential and to develop policies ensuring AI benefits all, not just the few.
